Turning Agricultural Waste Into Sustainable Art Materials

The Problem

Agricultural waste like rice husks and coconut fibers is often burned or discarded, causing pollution and wasted value. Meanwhile, students and artists rely on costly, non-biodegradable art materials.

Farm by-products are undervalued, art materials harm the environment, and communities miss income opportunities.

Students and artists lack affordable eco-friendly materials, farmers lose income from wasted by-products, and communities face pollution. Manufacturers, environmental groups, and consumers are also impacted, making sustainable art materials relevant to all stakeholders.

The problem exists because conventional art materials use unsustainable resources, while agricultural by-products are wasted due to low awareness, limited recycling infrastructure, and insufficient demand for eco-friendly alternatives.

As a team, we want to work on this problem because we are passionate about art, sustainability, and supporting local communities. Turning agricultural waste into eco-friendly art materials allows us to create positive environmental impact while empowering farmers, artists, and consumers.

We turn agricultural waste into sustainable art materials to reduce environmental harm from conventional supplies.

Conventional art materials harm the environment, waste farmers' by-products, and limit eco-friendly options for artists, communities, and consumers.
